🟡 Policy Watch 1

Regulatory developments affecting positioning

MEDIUMGreat British Energy · Dec 2025
GB Energy Strategic Plan: generation focus, not retail
▾

Great British Energy published its first Strategic Plan targeting 15GW of clean generation and storage capacity by 2030, with £8.3bn committed. Crucially, GBE will develop and own generation assets — it will not supply electricity directly to households or businesses. This is strategically positive for tem: the publicly-owned company builds supply, but the retail transaction layer remains open for private innovation. Content angle: 'GB Energy generates. tem delivers.'

Action →Content opportunity — position tem as the retail complement to GB Energy
🔗 Source: gbe.gov.uk
MEDIUMEDF/Ofgem · 2026
Ofgem to directly regulate energy brokers (TPIs)
▾

Ofgem has confirmed it will directly regulate Third Party Intermediaries (energy brokers) under a new compliance regime. This is a major shift — previously brokers operated with minimal oversight, contributing to the opaque pricing and hidden markups that inflate business energy costs. For tem, this validates the core thesis that the middleman layer is broken. As brokers face new regulatory costs, tem's direct-from-generator model becomes even more competitive.

Action →Monitor implementation timeline — prepare messaging on broker regulation
🔗 Source: edfenergy.com
HIGHCornwall Insight · Jan 2026
Non-commodity charges to reach 60% of business electricity bills
▾

Cornwall Insight forecasts that by 2026, non-commodity charges (network costs, policy levies, capacity market) will make up nearly 60% of a typical business electricity bill. This means wholesale energy costs are only 40% of what businesses pay — the rest is the system around it. This is a powerful data point for tem's messaging: even as renewable generation gets cheaper, the legacy transaction infrastructure keeps bills high.

Action →Use in content — '60% of your energy bill isn't energy'
🔗 Source: cornwall-insight.com
🔵 Competitor Moves 1

What others in the market did this week

HIGHOctopus Energy · Ongoing
Octopus Energy: Tracker tariff and business expansion
▾

Octopus continues to expand its transparent pricing products, including Tracker (daily wholesale-linked rates) and business-facing flexible green tariffs. Their Tracker tariff updates prices daily based on independently published wholesale costs and is marketed as giving customers visibility into the 'true cost of energy.' This is a direct narrative competitor to tem's transparency positioning — though the key distinction is that Octopus passes through wholesale market prices, while tem bypasses wholesale entirely via direct generator matching.

Action →Develop clear comparison messaging: wholesale pass-through vs direct-from-generator
🔗 Source: octopus.energy
MEDIUMCornwall Insight · Jan 2026
Behind-the-meter solar and storage boom: 1GW+ expected in 2026
▾

Cornwall Insight expects more than 1GW of new behind-the-meter solar and battery storage from businesses in 2026, as companies try to cut costs and sidestep grid connection delays. This represents a different kind of competitor to tem: businesses taking energy supply into their own hands rather than trusting any supplier. tem should consider how RED complements on-site generation — positioning as the best route-to-market for remaining grid needs.

Action →Explore hybrid positioning: RED + on-site generation = full energy independence
🔗 Source: cornwall-insight.com

How the public conversation is shifting

HIGHOfgem / Cornwall Insight · This week
Dominant frame: bills falling, but business costs still high
▾

The Q2 2026 price cap reduction is getting positive domestic coverage, but business energy remains a pressure point. Cornwall Insight notes that non-commodity charges are now the majority of business bills, and Ofgem's own research shows majority business concern about costs. The narrative gap — 'things are getting better for households, but not for businesses' — is tem's sweet spot. The inefficiency in the middle is exactly what RED eliminates.

Action →Data visualisation — wholesale price vs business bill vs RED pricing

MEDIUMGreat British Energy · Emerging trend
GB Energy and 'public ownership' shifting energy discourse
▾

The launch of GB Energy is shifting public conversation toward the idea that energy should serve public interest, not just private profit. GBE's messaging emphasises 'Clean, Secure, Yours' — a values-driven frame. tem's own brand narrative aligns: transparency, fairness, power back to customers. But there's a risk that 'public ownership' becomes the dominant solution frame, crowding out private innovation. tem should actively position as the private-sector complement: 'GB Energy owns the generation. tem democratises the transaction.'

Action →Refine positioning vis-à-vis GB Energy — complementary, not competitive
